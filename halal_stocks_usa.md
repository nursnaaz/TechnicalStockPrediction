## Shariah Screening Criteria Summary

For a stock to be considered **Halal**, it must pass both:

### 1. Business Activity Screen (Qualitative)
The company must NOT be primarily involved in:
- Conventional banking or insurance
- Alcohol production or distribution
- Tobacco production or distribution
- Gambling or gaming (casinos)
- Pork products
- Adult entertainment or pornography
- Weapons manufacturing (controversial)
- Interest-based financial services

### 2. Financial Ratios Screen (Quantitative - AAOIFI Standards)
- **Debt ratio:** Total interest-bearing debt / Market capitalization < 30%
- **Interest income ratio:** Interest income / Total revenue < 5%
- **Impermissible income ratio:** Non-compliant income / Total revenue < 5%
- **Cash & interest-bearing securities:** < 30% of market capitalization

## Grading System Explained

- **Halal A/A-:** Highly compliant, minimal concerns (<1% non-compliant revenue)
- **Halal B+/B:** Compliant but with moderate exposure (1-3% non-compliant revenue)
- **Halal C+/C:** Compliant but higher concerns (3-5% non-compliant revenue)
- **Doubtful:** Near threshold limits, requires careful monitoring
- **Haram:** Not Shariah-compliant, prohibited for Muslim investors
